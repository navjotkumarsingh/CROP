Cropping pattern refers to the type and sequence of crops grown on a particular land. Understanding these patterns helps improve productivity, soil health, and water management. Common patterns include:

  • Monocropping: Growing the same crop year after year.
  • Mixed Cropping: Cultivating two or more crops simultaneously on the same land.
  • Crop Rotation: Alternating crops in a planned sequence to preserve soil nutrients.
  • Intercropping: Growing two or more crops together with specific spacing arrangements.
